Connecting NiceEval is not all-or-nothing. Each tier adds one investment and unlocks another set of checks. Existing evals keep working as you move up.

| Tier | What you add | What you get | When to use it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1: send | An adapter with `send(input, ctx)` | Text assertions, structured output, hand-mapped tool events, multi-turn, HITL if implemented | First integration, or apps without tracing |
| Tier 2: send + OTel | Your app sends spans to NiceEval | Tool events derived from spans, usage, trace waterfall, less manual mapping | Apps already instrumented with AI SDK, LangSmith, OpenInference, OpenLLMetry, or GenAI semconv |
| Tier 3: flags | Your app exposes feature switches through `ctx.flags` | A/B comparisons of prompts, tools, retrieval, routing, or product variants | When you want to compare internal behavior, not only models |

## Tier 1

The adapter calls the interface your app already exposes and returns a `Turn`. You can start with only `status` and a `message` event, then add sessions, events, and HITL incrementally.

## Tier 2

Keep the same `send`, but add `events: otelEvents()` and forward `ctx.telemetry.headers` to the app. NiceEval receives spans, attributes them to a turn, derives standard events, and draws the waterfall in `niceeval view`.

## Tier 3

Expose the behavior you want to compare as experiment `flags`. NiceEval passes them through `ctx.flags`; your adapter forwards them to the app; the app decides what to change.
